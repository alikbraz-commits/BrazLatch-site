# Outbound prospect scoring rubric — 0 to 10

Apply to every candidate `prospect-researcher` produces. Same rubric
across all three territories.

## Positive factors

| Factor                                                                | Points |
|-----------------------------------------------------------------------|--------|
| Multi-location footprint (≥ 3 stores) or significant online reach     | +3     |
| Operates in a top vertical (equestrian retail, gate/fence hardware, dairy/livestock, rural/agri hardware) | +2 |
| Specialist in latches / gate hardware / stable hardware specifically  | +2     |
| 10+ years in market (longevity signal)                                | +1     |
| Family-owned or founder-led (Alik prefers — peer to peer)             | +1     |
| Listed as stockist for known premium hardware brands                  | +1     |
| Direct contact path (named buyer, email or LinkedIn URL captured)     | +1     |
| Imports direct from manufacturers today (skips an intermediary)       | +1     |
| OEM/private-label capability — relevant for India                     | +1     |
| Public revenue or store count suggests > 1,000 unit-per-quarter capacity | +2  |

## Penalties

| Red flag                                                              | Points |
|-----------------------------------------------------------------------|--------|
| Territory is already covered (US/CA/established EU)                   | −10    |
| Sells direct-competitor specialty product (e.g. another sequence latch) — possible conflict | −2 |
| Single-location independent (we want distribution leverage)           | −2     |
| Pure end-consumer / club / school (not a reseller)                    | −5     |
| Defunct or no recent web activity                                     | −10    |

## Tier mapping

| Score | Tier      | Action                                                    |
|-------|-----------|-----------------------------------------------------------|
| 10    | 🟢 Slam dunk | Personal email from Alik this week, offer sample shipment |
| 8–9   | 🟢 Strong   | Personalized templated email this month, qualifying Q     |
| 5–7   | 🟡 Worth a touch | Lightweight templated outreach with one qualifying Q  |
| 1–4   | 🔴 Distant  | Park. Revisit next quarter if Tier 1/2 are exhausted      |
| 0     | ⚫ No fit   | Remove and log reason                                     |

## Worked examples

### Example 1 — Naylors Equestrian (UK)
- Multi-location: 46 stores → **+3**
- Equestrian retail vertical → **+2**
- Not a hardware specialist specifically → **0**
- Decades in market (Naylors family established long-running brand) → **+1**
- Family-owned → **+1**
- Stockist of premium brands → **+1**
- Contact path: store finder + careers email visible → **+1**
- Direct importer (mainstream chain, likely yes) → **+1**
- OEM: probably no for this kind of product → **0**
- Store count × £ → > 1,000 units/quarter capacity easily → **+2**
- **Total: 12 → capped at 10** ✓

### Example 2 — Greg Grant Saddlery (AU)
- Multi-location: 4 stores → **+3**
- Equestrian retail vertical → **+2**
- Not a hardware specialist → **0**
- Established 1973 → **+1**
- Family-owned ("50 years family-owned") → **+1**
- Premium positioning, stocks leading brands → **+1**
- Contact path: brisbane HQ contact → **+1**
- Likely importer → **+1**
- Premium AU saddlery — under 1,000 units/qtr likely → **+0** (cap conservative)
- **Total: 10** ✓

### Example 3 — Single-location independent NZ saddlery
- Single location → **−2**
- Equestrian vertical → **+2**
- 5 years in market → **+0**
- Otherwise small → **+0**
- **Total: 0–2 → 🔴 Distant** ✓

## Notes for India-specific scoring

For Indian candidates, the OEM/private-label factor is a stronger
positive than for AU/UK. If the candidate is a manufacturer
(JPC Equestrian, Maanvi Exports, etc.) who also has a domestic
distribution arm, they fit two slots:

- as a **distributor** for a branded BrazLatch SKU
- as a **partner** for a co-branded or private-label OEM product

Score them on the distributor track but note OEM possibility under
`Why-fit` and `Notes`.
